The Gods

The Daegian

The gods of the dawn, these are the gods created at the dawn of the world, after the Firistwinter had ended. Yagma and Rim were the first gods, created from the dreams of the sleeping god Iss. Together, they created the rest of the Daegian, and together, the Daegian created the world as it is today.

    Estror, the Dawn Goddess. She guided the world in its infancy, and now cares for the newborn.
    Gitriw, Truth Speaker. Bound to speak only the truth, Gitriw is invoked at hundred meets when justice is sought.
    Haltan, King of the Gods. God of the sky, and ruler of the gods, Haltan is god of law and order. It was he who slew Rorn, his half-brother, who was previously considered to be lord over the gods.
    Moern, Sea Father. Morn is the endless deep, the lurker below, who sleeps beneath the oceans of the world. When arosed, he is angry and violent, and those who travel on his waters soon learn how to placate him.
    Reas, the Craftsman. Reas is a skilled craftsman, a creator of beauty. He is a smith and a bard, capable of crafting beauty from words as well as from metals.
    Rim, God of the Dead. The first of the gods, he and Yagma created the rest of the world. Rim dwells beneath the earth, and safe guards the souls of the dead ready for their resurrection.
    Thot, the Raven. He is said to be the craftiest of the gods. He sees and hears much that goes on in the world, and sometimes reports such findings to the gods, if it suits him. He is closely allied to Haltan, and is often his messenger.
    Yagma, Earth Mother. Created first along with Rim, Yagma created the world and all that is in it. She is the goddess of the eternal cycle of life, from birth to death.

The Younger Gods

The first of the younger gods was Rorn, born out of the womb of Yagma, fathered by Rim. Rorn was the first god to be born, rather than made, and the rest of the younger gods are likewise descended from other gods.

    Besenreth, Fenya's Shadow. Few have heard of Besenreth, though the rangers of Tuffin Wood say she is the sister of Fenya. She can be found wherever the light of Fenya does not fall. She is the goddess of secret places and unknown paths.
    Bis, the Sun God. Bis drives the chariot which pulls the sun around the world.
    Fenya, Moon Daughter. After the death of Rorn, she took up the guiding of the moon on its course. She now sits within the Moon, and watches the world. It is said that the Moon became beautiful when she took up residence.
    Gyltith, the Sorrowful. Daughter of Rorn and Estror, Gyltith is cursed with seeing the future. She can only see what she does not want to see however.
    Hreth, Winter's Cloak. Daughter of Rorn and Estror, she is a feared and terrible god, who sold her soul to the Ice during the Age of Winter.
    Neya, the Keeper of Oaths. Daughter of Haltan and Asabeth, she is the patron goddess of the Queen of the Kingdom of Rorn.
    Rorn the Wanderer. Created as a prototype human, the rest of mankind was made in his image. He became lord of the gods, until he was betrayed and slain by his own half-brother, Haltan.

Other Gods

    Asabeth, the Warrior. Asabeth is an eastern god, who came to the west many years after Rorn had travelled that path. She met Haltan, and bested him, taking him hostage for a year and forcing him to be her husband. She is a great warrior, though full of anger and jealousy.
    Meam, the Outcast. Son of Haltan, Meam's mother was a cow. He is an ugly, bestial creature, scorned by the rest of the gods. Dwelling on the edge of civilisation, he has rejected the ways of civilisation, and delights in the abuse and pain of others.
    Pel, the Perfect God. Created by Haltan and Reas during a contest with the giant Brokki. He is utterly beautiful, and is said to be the greatest work of craftmanship ever to come out of Reas' hands.
    Tharos, Night Stalker. He came with Asabeth from the east, but never joined the other gods. He spurns friends and family, and lives alone, surviving on his own wits. He has shown himself to be untrustworthy, capable of murder and theft.
    Walgi, Wolf Mother. No-one knows where Walgi came from, but she is the animal god, self-proclaimed mother of all the animals, especially those which hunt man.

Non-Gods

    Brokki, Giant King.
    Farfthi, the Greatest Dragon.
