Meam the Outcast

When Asabeth was trying to kill Rorn so that her husband could become lord of the gods, she tricked Rorn and Haltan into going off on a suicidal quest to retrieve the cursed sword Helvanger. She needed to distract Haltan from the quest however, so caused a cow to take the shape of a beautiful woman. The woman waylayed Haltan, and Rorn completed the quest on his own. From the union of Haltan and the nameless cow, sprang Meam - half god half beast, who grew to maturity within a year, raised by his mother.

Meam was born and grew up in the years following the death of Rorn, when the land was broken, and food was scarce. At the end of the first year, Meam was forced to eat his mother, for there was no other food in the valley where he lived. Not knowing who he was, or who his father was, Meam set off into the world to find out the answers to both questions.

Meam came across a small village, which he entered openely, seeking food and shelter. As soon as he was seen, the villagers took up stones and sticks to drive him from their village. Meam was half a god, but he was also only half a man, and half a beast as well. Cloven hooves, jutting jaw, bulging veins and miscoloured skin are only some of the disfigurements Meam suffered from. Going from village to village, Meam was driven out again and again.

Eventually, he came to a cave in a forest, where an old man sat. The old man did not shy away from the sight of Meam, but instead offered him food and shelter.

"Who am I?" Asks Meam of the old man.

"You are the son of the god Haltan. He created you, and it is he who owes you sanctuary."

Meam went looking for his father. Laughed at by gods. Thieving on edge of civilisation. Comes across two sisters, rapes and kills them. They are daughters of Bis.

Cults of Meam

Meam cultists are generally made up of sadists and sociopaths, often those who have themselves been spurned by society, either through their own actions, or because of no fault of their own.

Cults are depressingly common, but tend to be small and disorganised. Generally, whoever is the biggest bully gets to be 'high priest', and anything else depends on that person's ability to enforce their will.

It is said that the spirit of Meam enters those who venerate his name, and who mark his symbol upon their bodies. It is definitely the case that many followers of Meam are incredibly deranged, engaging in acts of sadism and masochism, including murder, rape, self-mutilation, torture and even cannibalism (to honour Meam's consumption of his own mother). Many followers of Meam eventually degenerate into ghouls, though few survive long enough.
