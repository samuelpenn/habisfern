The Gods of Rorn

The gods feature heavily in the life of the people, for all that happens does so because of the gods. If the gods are not placated, then fields will become infertile, storms will ravish the countryside, and people will die. Few of the gods are malicious though. Nevertheless, people placate them just to ensure that they keep on their good side.

Asabeth

Asabeth is a powerful goddess of war and revenge. She demands the sacrifice of prisoners taken in war (generally, by nailing them to a tree). Long ago, when men still dwelt in Tarvallah, she came across with the Eastlings (those men who came after the followers of Rorn, and dwelt to the east of Tarvallah, and who did not look to Rorn as their god).

Asabeth fought with Haltan at the borders of Tarvallah, and she prevailed, taking Haltan as her prisoner, chaining him in his own hall. She raped him, and kept him as prisoner until after the birth of their children (Fenya, Bis and Neya). She freed Haltan, and gave herself to him as his wife. She deserted her people, and left them to suffer outside Tarvallah.

She is a warrior goddess, quick to anger, and eager for the death of her enemies. Asabeth is considered to be barbaric by most people, though various cults to her have risen up over time, most notably in Estwed.

Bis

God of the sun, mischief and chaos. He drives the chariot which pulls the sun around the world once a day, but sometimes strays from his path.

Estror

She is the goddess of dawn, spring and new life. While Yagma grants the conception of a child, Estror will look after the newborn.

Fenya

She is the goddess who took up the task of leading the moon in its course around the world after the death of Rim. She is goddess of the moon, beauty and the night. She is the brother of Bis, and the daughter of Rorn and Neya. She is also seen as the huntress.

Haltan

Haltan is the god of the Sky, storms, war and justice. Though he was defeated by Asabeth, he is considered to be the most skilled of the gods at war, and is thereby favoured by warriors. His wisdom and general desire for justice also makes him a lot more respectable than his wife. He is the most powerful of the male gods.

He was brother to Rorn, though at the time of the Betrayal, it was he who challenged Rorn (after being goaded into doing so by Asabeth), and slew him in combat. Because of Rorn‘s death, men were forced out of Tarvallah, into the less fertile lands to the north.

He wields the great sword Helvanger.

Gitriw

One of the original gods of the Daegian, she taught truth and cooperation to the animals of the world during its dawn. The gods and the Giants lay bindings of truth upon her in order such that she could fairly judge a contest between Brokki and Haltan, and from that day on, has never been able to tell a lie.

Gyltith

She is the silent witness, goddess of sorrow and remorse. Her guidance is sought by those who wish for different pasts or futures. She is also the goddess of both prophecy and hindsight.

Hreth

She is the goddess of winter, the cold wind and the bringer of snow. Generally considered too close to the Ice to be friendly, she is called upon more to stay away than anything. Her form is that of a great winged beast who can cover the world with her snowy wings.

Meam

Meam is a demi-god, born out of the unnatural union of Haltan and a cow. He is an ugly, deformed monster, often pitiful, but incredibly strong. His own offspring, of which there are many, are the ettin.

Neya

Neya is the goddess of peace, serenity and marriage. She is the Keeper of Oaths, and the vengeance which strikes down those that break oaths made on her name is the only time she shows anger or violence.

Her priestesses look after the Queen of Rorn, and decide the succession.

Pel

Pel was created by Haltan and Reas, and in attempt to craft the most perfect person in order to win the secrets of weapon making from the Giant god Brokki. Pel is beautiful, being physically perfect in all ways. However, he is somewhat shallow of personality, and very vain.

Rim

The god of death, he lives beneath the world, and gathers in the souls of the dead. It is also his task to pass out souls to the newborn though, so as well as being god of death, he is also god of life. To this end, he has a close relationship with Yagma.

Rim, along with Yagma, was the first god, created from the dreams of Iss.

Rorn

Rorn the Wanderer was the moon god, who watched against the coming of the Ice when men dwelt in the East. His distraction from his task by a mortal woman cause the fall of man, and his guilt led him to lead as many as he could to safety, eventually bringing his followers to the fertile lands of Tarvallah.

He was a watcher, though beset by wander lust. He was killed by Haltan in single combat.

Thot

The Raven, and messenger of the gods, Thot sees everything that goes on in the world. He is as swift as the gale, capable of being anywhere in the land in no time at all. Clever and crafty, he can also be a trickster, spy and blackmailer.

Walgi

The Wolf Mother, guardian of the beasts and huntress of man. Though she is considered to be the enemy of men, she is seen as an honourable foe, one who is to be respected rather than feared.

Those who hunt her children often call on her before hand, to declare their intentions so that she may look kindly on them. To those who play by her rules, she is a good loser. To those who abuse her children, and mock her, her vengeance is swift.

Yagma

She is the Earth Mother, the most powerful of the gods. She is a fertility goddess, who bargains with Rim for the souls for all newborn life. She is petitioned for conception, pregnancy, agriculture, rain and the harvest.

Yagma is as much though a goddess of Death as she is of Life. It is she who turns the wheel of time, that eventually brings all things full circle.
