Interests
Asabeth is a warrior goddess, skilled in sword and spear, and filled with a passion for war and death. She is the goddess of blood feuds, of fighting because of hatred, anger or a simple passion for violence.
Worshippers
Worshippers of Asabeth are few. Most who follow the warrior path choose Haltan as their patron, since he is the god of honourable war. The majority of Asabeth followers are female warriors, though even here she is rarely found.
Stories
The Capture of Haltan. When Asabeth first came to the west, she fought and defeated Haltan, making him her husband.
The Death of Rorn. Though it was Haltan who killed Rorn, it was Asabeth who put him up to it, though without his knowledge.
The Warriors of Estwed. Though not directly involving Asabeth, this tells of a group of her worshippers - a cult of warrior women - who took over the rule of the Kingdom of Estwed long ago.

Asabeth, the Warrior

Asabeth is unrelated to the rest of the gods - she came from the East while mankind was living in Tarvallah. She came with the Eastlings (those men who came after the followers of Rorn, and dwelt to the east of Tarvallah, and who did not look to Rorn as their god).

Asabeth is a powerful goddess of war and revenge. She demands the sacrifice of prisoners taken in war (generally, by nailing them to a tree).

Asabeth fought with Haltan at the borders of Tarvallah, and she prevailed, taking Haltan as her prisoner, chaining him in his own hall. She raped him, and kept him as prisoner until after the birth of their children (Fenya, Bis and Neya). She freed Haltan, and gave herself to him as his wife. She deserted her people, and left them to suffer outside Tarvallah.

She is a warrior goddess, quick to anger, and eager for the death of her enemies. Asabeth is considered to be barbaric by most people, though various cults to her have risen up over time, most notably in Estwed.
