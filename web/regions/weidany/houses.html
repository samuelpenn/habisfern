Feuding Between Houses

Feuds between the various houses are quite common, though most of the time relationships are kept to the odd border skirmish or political manouevering at court. It is not unheard of for one House to send a small band of warriors half way across the Kingdom to cause trouble on the lands of another.


The Extent of Houses

A House consists of its Ealdorman, plus his relatives. Quite how close a relative has to be tends to vary, and a favoured distant cousin can sometimes be given higher status than a sibling or child. Generally, anyone within two generations (descended from the ealdorman's grandfather) will be considered a proper member of the House, and anyone within four generations can be almost certain to claim membership. Geneaology is measured always through the male side of the family.

The Noble Houses

Originally, Weidany was ruled by many Kings, whose kingdoms warred with one another, until the weaker kingdoms were absorbed into the stronger, and the current state of a single Kingdom was reached.

The blood lines of these old royal families still exist, and many have a great deal of political power within the Kingdom. As before, there are rivalries, alliances and outright hatred between these lines. Today, they are known as the Noble Houses. Any ealdorman (a noble with a thousand hides or more of hereditary lands) can consider himself to be part of a noble House. Any noble related to such a person within six generations can also consider themselves to be part of that House.

House of Aeddan

This was once one of the most powerful of the Weidanic blood lines, but now there are few who claim membership of it. It was magically strong, and had connections to the Elder Races, but now owns little land, and has even less political power.

Members of the House have a tradition of choosing Weidanic names, rather than Rornic, though this isn't always observed.

It is allied with the House of Oswald, though this is thought more to be for reasons of survival, than genuine friendship.

Ealdorman Uwain is lord of this House.

House of Aethlfryth

This is the House of the current royal family. Only females are ever born to this House, and it is traditional for those who want a chance at the throne to never take a husband.

Many of this family who are not close to the throne form the elite royal guard, or join the priest hood of Neya.

House of Caewlin

Caewlin was the first of the Rornic kings of Weidany, and it was his line that ruled the Kingdom of Lanuch before the rise of the House of Aethlfryth. Perhaps because of this, there is a lot of enmity between the two Houses, with the nobles of Lanuch desiring much greater dominance than they currently have.

House of Cenic

This is the youngest of the Houses, formed only in 1207 when the thegn Cenic was awarded land of the new shire of Cenicsdyke after its namesake led several very successful campaigns against the invading short folk.

Since less than a generation has passed since its formation, there is still a great deal of jockeying for power within the family ranks.

House of Eanwulf

The House was once the rulers of the Kingdom of Estwed, in the days before the rise of the Cult of Asabeth in those lands. It has regained some of its power and influence since those glory days, but not all.

House of Erwin

This is actually the parent House of House Aethlfryth. It is closely allied with that House, and supports it in every way possible. This loyalty has meant the House has amassed large amounts of lands around its ancestral home lands of Merrick.

House of Gwern

The ancient blood line of this House once controlled most of Weidany, naming itself the Kingdom of Aidan. It‘s attempts at further expansion were halted by Aethelwulf, and later Aethlfryth. It‘s kingdom was shattered, and the all principle members of its family were slain.

Today, the family barely constitutes a noble House, having a few lands on the borders of the Kingdom in Aidan shire. The family is still bitter about its defeat at the hands of the House of Aethlfryth, and is generally considered treacherous, dishonourable and decadent.

For the past few generations, not a single member of the House capable of influencing decisions has ever shown a hint of subtlety however.

The current head of the House, Ealdorman Caradoc, is halfway to madness and spends much of his time with his harem of slave girls.

House of Hrulgar

In 1107, the thegn Hrulgar was granted large amounts of bookland in the shires of Estwed, Eastmarch and Southwood, making Hrulgar an Ealdorman, and forming the House of Hrulgar. The reasons for this were entirely due to favouritism from the Queen, a fact which did not go unnoticed by the other Houses, and the House is still despised to this day.

For the most part, its members have always looked eastward, towards expansion further into Nailand, rather than inward against the rival Houses.

House of Merin

A minor House that is based in the shire of Talmoth. It is closely allied with the House of Caewlin, though out of fear more than anything else. It tries its best to take the most conservative decision, to stay out of the notice of everyone.

House of Oswald

Most of the lands of this House are found in Angsly, around the old Kingdom of Aeddan. This House has much magical might, and some rumours say that it has ties to the House of Aeddan.

Its central family is quite secretive in its ambitions, goals and actions, and tends to stay away from a lot of the political manoeuvring in the royal court. The head of the House, Ealdorman Eadric is reputed to be a wizard, as well as a capable warrior and noble.

House of Peada

The House of the old kings of Estwed, it is today fairly neutral in its relationships with the royal House, and is more concerned with expanding its domains eastward into Nailand.

House of Wihtgar

Once a powerful House whose kings were part of the Council of Kings. It's lands were lost during the war with Merkia, and even the name of its old kingdom - that of Perrin - no longer exists. Today it is a weak house, dreaming of days gone by.
