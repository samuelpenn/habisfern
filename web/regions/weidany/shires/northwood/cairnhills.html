Who Lives Here
Around the edges of the hills can be found several human villages, the largest of which is Rorn's Vale. The central portions of the hills have no permanent inhabitants, and is declared to be forest land by the Queen.

Who Rules
The hills come under the authority of the hundred of Rorn's Vale, though for the most part the law is considered unenforceable up on the hills themselves. Unless something is causing trouble for the villages nearby, anything up on the hills is left free to do as it pleases.

The Riders
One of the most common legends about the hills is that of the Riders - warrior horsemen who, it is said, are doomed to ride eternally, never setting foot on land. When the lands were ruled by barbarians, there was a king called Enstig Longhair, who ruled the lands around the Cairn Hills. Legend has it that he married a woman of the otherlands, a fey beauty who spirited him and his huscarls off to her home to live for eternity. Every now and again, him and his men return to patrol their original kingdom. If they ever set a foot on the earth, then the years they have cheated will catch up with them, and they will die.

Rorn's Vale

Rorn's Vale is a typical hundred town, with a population of about four hundred. The thegn here, Berdun, is an old, somewhat bitter man who now lives alone, the rest of his family having either died or left him. His mood has infected much of the village. The reeve is Jarn Hastikson, also a priest of Ashek.

Jarn is old, mad, and somewhat incoherent. During his saner moments he can be very perceptive and crafty however. He is a wizard, of quite a bit of power, though has forgotton almost as much as he has learned, His specialities are men, animals and weather.

The most respected member of the community is Jangi, daughter of Jarn, though no-one knows her mother. She turned up thirty years ago in Jarn's house, with Jarn claiming he was her father. She is unmarried. She has black hair, green eyes and very pale skin. When she was 12, she went missing for several days, and when she was found she had tatoos across her chest, left shouldre and back, and wouldn't say what had happened.

The Cairns

Spread across the southern hills in a rough east - west line are the cairns. There are about four dozen in all, though legend has it that their true number cannot be accurately counted. There are six major cairns, which each have extensive crypts beneath them. The rest are single caves, or simple mounds containing no more than one or two bodies.

The Round Chamber

A tunnel goes directly into a steep hillside - getting to it can be difficult. There is a large round chamber within, with some smaller chambers off from the main one.

Ecbrit's Cairn

The Ecbrit in question was a recent thegn who had himself buried here along with the other warriors. His tormented ghost is often seen near to the site. It is a simple mound, but has the most extensive tunnel system of any of the cairns.

The White Man

A chalk man with spear is carved into the top of this hill. There is an entrance beneath the man's feet.

Stone Shield Hill

A mound fifteen metres long, six metres wide and some three metres tall. It's sides are riged with flat, circular stones, and there is a stone archway leading into a narrow chamber at the east end.

Corpse Hill

A very large artificial hill.

Four Barrows

Four barrows aligned facing towards each other.

Ashek's Tower

The strangest feature of the hills is that of Ashek's Tower. It rises 25m above ground level as a steep hill, the top of which opens into a rocky shaft which drops about 150m almost straight down to a small, subterranean lake.
