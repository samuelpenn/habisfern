Predating Cerdic

The Scrolls were begun in the 687th year following the death of Rorn. It was Embryt who worked out this date, for previously all years had been counted according to the current local king. For the first few centuriees, Embryt's system of counting the years was found only within the scrolls.

Gradually, it has come to be used more commonly, though many people still prefer to count the years from some local event.

Previous to 687, the dates have been pieced together, mostly by word of mouth accounts. The further back one goes into the past, the more likely that the dates, and even order of events, are wrong.



Chronicler Bias

Embryt wrote down the facts as he could ascertain them, and many of his successors have tried to emulate this style. Over time, several chroniclers have added their own biases into the writings though, and it is sometimes difficult to tell the fact from the personal views of the author.

The Early Centuries

There is betrayal amongst the gods, and Rorn, god of the people, is slain. A great curse is laid upon the lands of Tarvallah, and the People of Rorn are driven out by pestilence and famine. The people flee to the east into unknown lands, and to the north of the land of Weidany.

217

Caewlin fights against Seaxmund, a great king of the Weidan, and slays him, taking the place of Saxmundham as his own, becoming the first of the people to be King over the Weidan.

420

The king Brytweider takes many men north across barbarian lands to found a kingdom on the coast.
