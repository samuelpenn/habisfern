Social Glossary

The Queen: It is she who owns the majority of the land in the Kingdom, and all its people are subject to her, either directly, or indirectly.

Ealdormen: Nobles who own large tracts of land in their own right. They wield a great deal of power, and are effective rulers in their own right over the lands they own.

Thegns: A noble who has been loaned land by the Queen for his lifetime. They owe fyrd service, and it is their responsibility to keep roads, forts and bridges in good repair.

Reeves: A representative of the Queen, a reeve presides at the hundred courts to administer over arguments and collect fines.

Ceorl: A freeman, who farms a hide of land from the Queen.

Kotsetla: A very poor freeman, who works their lord's land because their own is not enough to support their family.

Slaves: An unfree person who is owned by a freeman. They may own property themselves.

Outlaws: Anyone who has been cast outside the law due to crimes they committed. It is illegal to seek revenge for the killing of an outlaw.


Land Glossary

Shire: An administrative unit of land used in the Kingdom for purposes of law and tax. Each has its own court, over seen by a shire-reeve, who collects taxes and sees that the Queen's laws are carried out.

Hundred: One hundred hides of land. A unit for legislative purposes.

Hide: An amount of land capable of supporting an extended family. This is around thirty people. It is about equivalent to 120 acres, though is a qualitative rather and quantitative measure. In the most fertile parts of the Kingdom, it can be as low as 40 acres.

4 acres: The amount of land required to support one person.

Manor: The demise of a thegn, and generally a number of people capable of supporting a thegn. Five hides is seen as that required to keep a thegn in the status to which he is expected to live, and to support his training, arms and armour, as well as war horses and anything else required to serve his Queen.

Society

The society is pre-feudal across most of Rorn.

Those Who Rule

Her Majesty is the final arbitrator of law and justice within the Kingdom, though except in cases which directly affect the Crown, day to day cases of law are delegated to shire-reeves, each of whom acts as the Queen's authority in one of the nineteen shires of the Kingdom. In turn, the shire-reeves delegate to reeves, each of whom oversee the carrying out of the Queen's justice and authority for a hundred - about equivalent to a dozen villages.

It is the job of the shire-reeves, and the reeves, to collect fines and taxes, and to pass these on to the Queen (after taking a small share of course). Though the reeves and shire-reeves have total authority within their jurisdiction, their station is not hereditary, and neither do they own any of the land they oversee.

The protection of the land is entrusted to the thegns, who are warriors and lords, and to whom the government of all other aspects of the land is given. It is their duty to serve in the fyrd during times of war, and to keep forts, roads and bridges in good repair throughout the year.

In payment for these obligations, each thegn has right to the produce and slaves of the land they are granted. However, as for the reeves, the thegns do not own the land they govern, for it is still the property of the Crown. Though the land will often pass onto the chosen son of the thegn for purposes of convenience on the thegn's death, it is not always the case, and the Queen may pass it to any person she deems suitable if it is Her wish.

Ealdormen

Ideally, all of the land within the Kingdom is owned by the Queen, and a thegn may have their lands (known as laenland) taken back at whim. There are many nobles who resent this state of affairs, and over time, through one means or another (normally as reward for service above and beyond the call of duty), a thegn may be granted bookland, which comes with a hereditary right of ownership.

Such land is outside the Queen's control, and the noble may do with such land as he pleases. The rights of fines pass to the noble, not the Queen, and so he often has his own reeves. He still owns services to the Queen, and taxes, but such a noble has a great deal more power.

A thegn who owns more than a hundred of bookland is known as an ealdorman. The most powerful ealdormen form the great noble houses of the Kingdom.

Those Who Toil

Freemen

Ceorls

The majority of the people in the Kingdom are freemen - ceorls - who have been granted land by the Queen for their lifetime, which they work for their own profit. In return for this, they owe some obligations to the Queen - including fyrd service and food rent (known as feorm).

Generally, a ceorl will not be called up to the fyrd except in defence of his own shire, and few can afford to keep more than a spear and shield for such use.

Though, technically, a ceorl owes nothing to anyone save the Queen, this has been changing. For the most part, ceorls give feorm to a local thegn.

A ceorl will be the head of his household, and farm a hide of land.

Kotsetla

A kotsetla (or cottager) are barely free, and owe many obligations to the local thegn in return for support. A kotsetla may have five acres of land with which to support their family. They farm their lords demesne, and are granted a share of the food from that in order to make up the rest.

The Unfree

As well as the ceorl, there are the slaves, who have few rights of their own, and are considered to be the property of a freeman (either a ceorl, or more normally, a thegn). About 10% of the population of the Kingdom is made up of slaves.

Slaves are not totally without rights, and the more extreme forms of abuse by their owner are punishable by law. A slave may also earn money, and own goods, eventually buying their own freedom if they ever earn enough.

Those Who Pray

The gods of Weidany are many, and their demands of men can be a burden. Man though is the creation of the gods, and is therefore indebted to them. Further, without the gods, Man would have succumbed to the Powers of the Ice long ago, with the results of death, or servitude to it, far less preferable to anything the gods of Summer could ever require.

Since the death of Rorn, the most powerful of the gods has been Haltan, a warrior and law maker, who rules from his throne in the sky realm from where he can see the entire world.

All priests pay him some respect, though in varying degrees of reverence. Temples to him are places of rock and stone, often a stone table upon which justice is carried out. To this end, the local reeve often takes on the tasks required to serve Haltan.

Every village will have a priest of its own, who knows the lore of the gods, and who can call upon them, or placate them, when the need arises. Beyond the worship of Haltan, few of the priests dedicate themselves to but one god, since all the gods have influence on one part of a mans life or another.

Yagma is the mother of all things, and it is also she who guides the spirits of the dead into the hands of Rim, the god of the underworld where the spirits await reincarnation at a later time. Ensuring that the bodies of the dead are properly buried, to ensure easy transition of the spirit to the underworld, is one of the most important tasks a priest must perform.

Estror is the goddess of the dawn, and must be properly thanked on the birth of a child, or when seeds are sown at spring. Neya is the Keeper of Oaths, whose vengeance against those that break oaths given in her name - such as those of marriage - can be swift and terrible. There is a great temple devoted to Neya at Stowell, next to the royal palace, where the priestesses seek the protection of the royal line.

Ashek

During the winter at the start of 1057, peace was made with a great northern force which was led by the priests of a god called Ashek. The peace declared that the people of Ashek would become part of the Kingdom, but that the priests would be allowed to set up their own temples, and carry on the worship of their god.

The priests claim that Ashek is the one god, of whom all other gods are a reflection. He is the Mad God, and his priests seek greater understanding of him by delving into the arts of magic, which can only lead to madness for a mortal mind.

Currently, two great stone temples have been built to Ashek within the Kingdom - one at Stowell, the other at Cerdicsfield.
