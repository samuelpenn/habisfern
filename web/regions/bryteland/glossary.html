The Kingdom's View
The northwestern parts of the isle are considered by the Kingdom of Rorn to be barbaric nations, intent on raiding the richer lands to the south.

Glossary Bryteland

Alstoth

Landing place near the Als. A coastal town near the border of Northwood on the river Als. It has a population of about 1,200.

Austrvithr

East wood. The name given to the forests east of Glittertind. They are mostly deciduous, and the land is remarkably flat until it reaches the northern hills.

Brysvath

Ford on the Bryte River. A major town, and the largest in southeastern kingdoms. It has a population of about 3,000.

Ethelby

A town with a population of about 3000. It is the royal seat of Ethelred Haraldson.

Farnivithr

Woods in central Bryteland.

Glittertind

A large fortified town on the northwestern coast of Bryteland. It is stone walled, with a keep overlooking the harbour. It has a population of about 3,000.

Kord

A fortified town with a population of 1,800.

Kulvskogr

The name given to the dense woods which cover the southwestern coast of Bryteland. There are many inlets along this coast where small villages have grown up, each owning a longship which is used for raiding more southern lands.

Myrdal

A hill town, with a population of just under a thousand.

Novborg

A coastal town with a population of 2,200.

Raedby

The largest town in Bryteland, Raedby is a trade port of some importance, seeing ships from all along the western coasts. It has a population of approximately 5,000.

Saldonborg

A coastal town with a population of 1,500.

Tanwall (town)

A small town based around a stone keep in the Tanwall hills. The town overlooks the place where the Brytish were defeated in their war against Tanwall, and where an agreement was reached by the kings of Tanwall to form an alliance against the greater enemy. Tanwall town is part of Tanwall itself. It has a population of 600.

Thornvithr

Wood of Thorns. A dense forest in the northern hills, Thornvithr is practically uninhabited, and mostly unexplored.

Toatstath

A small town with a population of about 600.
